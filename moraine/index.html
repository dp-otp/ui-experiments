§ 01 — Dossier

Six garments, laid down in order, recorded by weight and origin.

Deposit 04 is the inaugural sediment laid down by Moraine. Each piece is constructed from a single bolt or yarn lot, finished by hand in workshops across Biella, Lerwick, Kyoto, and the North Norfolk coast. The deposit closes when the cloth is exhausted; nothing in this stratum is reproduced.

i.STRATUM

A finite sediment of cloth.

Six pieces, six hundred units. Each garment carries its stratum line — Quaternary scree, banded ironstone, halite crust — naming the geological era of its inspiration.

ii.REGISTRY

Catalogued, recorded, signed.

Every unit stamped DM-04.A.037 of 108. NFC chip embedded in the care label, scannable to verify against the locked archive.

iii.INHERITANCE

Built to outlast the buyer.

Lifetime free repair for any owner. A stitched linen logbook records the cloth's life — repair stamps, ownership transfers, and the events the wool has touched.

§ 06 — Inheritance

To deposit is to leave behind. Each piece is built to outlast its first wearer.

The book travels
with the cloth.

Every Moraine garment is issued with a stitched linen logbook in a hidden pocket. Repairs are carried out by the workshop without charge for the lifetime of the cloth, and the logbook is updated by hand at each return.

Garments are made to outlast the first owner and to be passed on with the book intact, so the next holder inherits the record as well as the wool. There are no seasons at Moraine, only deposits.
