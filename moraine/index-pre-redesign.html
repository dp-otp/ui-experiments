§ 01 — Dossier

Deposit 04 is the inaugural sediment laid down by Moraine and comprises six garments catalogued between November 2023 and August 2024. Each piece is constructed from a single bolt or yarn lot, recorded by weight and edition, and finished by hand in workshops across Biella, Lerwick, Kyoto, and the North Norfolk coast. The deposit is closed at 84 hero coats and tapers across the remaining five pieces; nothing in this stratum will be reproduced. Subsequent deposits will follow when the cloth is ready, not when the calendar is.

§ 04 — Inheritance

To deposit is to leave behind. Each piece is built to outlast its first wearer.

Every Moraine garment is issued with a stitched linen logbook that records its making, its owner, and the events of its life. Repairs are carried out by the workshop without charge for the lifetime of the cloth, and the logbook is updated by hand at each return. Garments are made to outlast the first owner and to be passed on with the book intact, so that the next holder inherits the record as well as the wool. There are no seasons at Moraine, only deposits, and no garment is retired while it can still be mended.

The book travels
with the cloth.

Every Moraine garment carries a stitched-in linen logbook in a hidden pocket. The first page records its making — cloth, mill, atelier, completion date, original owner. Subsequent pages record first wear, first repair, first journey, first ceremony. The final pages hold the ownership transfer record and the atelier's repair stamps.

The logbook never leaves the garment. When sent to Marche, Lerwick, or Norfolk for repair, the book travels with it — the repair atelier stamps the page. When the garment is passed on, the logbook is passed on. The unit code is the membership; whoever holds the cloth holds the rights.
