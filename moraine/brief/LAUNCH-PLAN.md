# MORAINE — Launch Plan

## 1. POSITIONING

Moraine is an archival garment house, not a fashion brand: each deposit is a numbered, finite sediment of cloth designed to be inherited, repaired, and logged across generations. Where The Row sells restraint and Lemaire sells line, Moraine sells provenance — every piece ships with a physical logbook that becomes the artefact alongside the garment. The competitive moat is not aesthetic but documentary: Moraine is the only house at this tier treating ownership history as part of the product.

## 2. DROP CADENCE

- **Frequency**: Three deposits per year. February, June, October. No exceptions, no surprise drops, no holiday capsules.
- **Why this cadence**: Italian/Portuguese small-batch mills run 14-18 week lead times on bespoke cloth orders below 200m. Three deposits leaves 16 weeks per cycle — enough for cloth → make → photography → release without compression. Faster cadence forces stock fabric and kills the archival premise.
- **Numbering**: DM-01 through DM-∞, sequential, never reset by year. Each garment within a deposit gets a sub-code: DM-04.A through DM-04.F. Individual units are stamped DM-04.A.037/108 (piece A, unit 37 of 108).
- **Restock stance**: Never. A sold-out deposit stays sold out. Inheritance program is the only route to acquire prior deposits. This protects the finite-sediment premise — restocking turns Moraine into a label.

## 6. PRICING JUSTIFICATION

**DM-04.A — Heavy Cashmere Coat (retail £2,200)**: cloth £576 / make £480 / trim £38 / logbook+packaging £42 / freight £18 / duty £110 = **landed £1,303 → 40.8% margin**.

**DM-04.D — Heavyweight Wool Trouser (retail £680)**: cloth £112 / make £130 / trim £14 / logbook+packaging £22 / freight £6 / duty £31 = **landed £315 → 53.7% margin**.

Blended deposit margin ~48% gross. After processing, shipping subsidy, returns (~6%), contribution lands ~38-42%. A sold-through deposit of 600 units across 6 SKUs grosses ~£540K, contributes ~£210K — funds DM-05's cloth order with runway.

## 7. INHERITANCE PROGRAM — MECHANICS

**Repair intake**: customer requests via web form with unit code + damage photo. Moraine issues prepaid DPD/UPS label within 48 hours. **Moraine pays inbound and outbound shipping for life of garment.**

**Repair scope**: unlimited structural repairs (seams, linings, button replacement, moth-hole reweaving, hem reset). Three free major restorations per garment lifetime. Cleaning not included. Turnaround 6-10 weeks.

**Inheritance route**: new owner submits unit code + one-line statement of how the garment came to them. Moraine transfers ownership in registry, mails a continuation logbook insert, activates repair access. No fee.

**Logbook**: 96-page linen-bound notebook, A6, Munken Pure 120gsm, sewn binding. Manufactured in Suffolk or Lithuania (~£14/unit at 600 run). First page: unit code, deposit number, cloth provenance, atelier name, completion date, original owner's name handwritten by founder. Pages 2-10: pre-printed prompts. Pages 11-90: blank ruled. Final pages: ownership transfer, repair log, atelier stamps. Logbook never leaves the garment — travels with it on every repair.

## 10. RISKS + WHAT KILLS THIS

1. **Cloth lead-time slip**: mill delays 4 weeks, drop missed. *Mitigation*: order DM-N+1 cloth the day DM-N closes — always two deposits in pipeline.
2. **Inheritance program economics**: lifetime free shipping + repair scales linearly. *Mitigation*: cap free major restorations at 3 per garment, ring-fence 8% of every deposit's revenue into a repair fund.
3. **Founder bottleneck**: personal DMs, hand-signed notes don't scale past ~2,000 customers. *Mitigation*: hire one operations lead at DM-08 or 1,500 customers.
4. **Counterfeiting**: fake unit codes will appear on Grailed. *Mitigation*: NFC chip in every garment's care label, scannable to verify against registry. ~£0.40/unit.
5. **Founder identity collapse into brand voice**: voice diversification before crisis. *Mitigation*: brand voice guide as standalone doc by DM-03; one journal entry per deposit guest-authored.
